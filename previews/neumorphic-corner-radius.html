Current: the lighter card gradient shows in the rounded corner gaps.
D — Surface matches bars: keeps the current shape but lifts the card surface to the indicator/hint bar tone, so the corner peek blends in and reads as intentional — no transparency, paddles keep their radius.
A — Hide behind piece: removes the contrasting surface, but the corners become transparent (the dashboard shows through) since the rounded paddles don't fill them.
B — Square paddle regions: the paddles extend into the corners and the card's overflow: hidden rounds them — clean edges, opaque surface, and it also removes the little inner-corner notch at the rocker seam. Usually the best standalone fix.
C — A + B: same clean corners as B; the surface is purely the paddles (matters only on translucent themes where you don't want any base fill).
E — Full-width paddles + faint bars: the paddles span edge-to-edge so they fill (and round) the corners with no inset gap; the indicator/hint bars become 90% transparent overlays. Clean corners while the bars stay visible as a subtle hint.
